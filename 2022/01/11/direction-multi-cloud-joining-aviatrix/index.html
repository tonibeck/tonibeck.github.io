Direction Multi-Cloud: Joining Aviatrix

2022-01-11 · 738 words · 4 minute read
aviatrix · networking · infrastructure

The cloud market is developing at great speed. I had two great, successful years as a Solution Architect at Microsoft. Microsoft has a fantastic team, great customers and outstanding momentum with Azure.

It was a great time with personal growth and definitely a whole lot of learnings. In 2022, however, I have decided to take the experience I have gained and transfer into a new role at Aviatrix in EMEA. Here is why I think this is the right move.

Direction: Multi-Cloud

Cloud is in full takeoff right now. Organizations are under pressure to define their cloud strategy and back it up with solid architecture.

One trend here is definitely Multi-Cloud. The best way I can describe this is with a short story from my experience at Microsoft:

​I supported one of my favourite Microsoft customers through his cloud journey, from zero to hero. At first they had a single-vendor strategy, but they quickly built up cloud expertise and then stepped on the gas. That was when they started looking towards AWS and GCP to cherrypick the best services. Next, they asked us, Microsoft, if we could help them with the network setup for that.

This opened my eyes to the fact that most organizations will soon be faced with connecting to more than one cloud provider. There is no single architecture here on the network side that can be replicated across multiple providers. Basically, each provider offers its own network stack.

There is a need to rethink the existing concepts and Aviatrix is leading the way in this area. Aviatrix offers a platform and architecture that is consistent across cloud service providers. In this way, the challenges can be addressed effectively and the infrastructure can be provided for the future in multi-cloud.

With that intro in mind, here are some other things to consider:

  • Visibility: The fact that networks no longer comprise just one provider means that there is a lack of visibility. The separate systems of the providers do not allow a uniform visibility dashboard to be provided for the individual connections. This challenge will become even greater if the trend towards multi-cloud continues and applications are hosted hybrid or by several different cloud providers.

  • Devops for Multi-Cloud Networking: Concepts such as infrastructure as code and automation need to be made usable in the multi-cloud environment. This is a challenge because each cloud is different and the concepts are therefore not adaptable. Aviatrix is the leader in this area too. With its Unified Platform, Aviatrix offers the right approach to implement Devops requirements in the multi-cloud landscape.

  • Security: Traditional security concepts need to be rethought in multi-cloud approaches. Simple “keep the bad stuff out” mechanisms no longer work in the increasingly complex landscape. Here too, Aviatrix has the right architecture with its unified platform. Security is implemented directly in the data plane and thus helps to better meet the challenges in the multi-cloud world.

Each of these is one of our focus areas - so I’ll get into that in further blogs soon.

We are ready to lead these discussions, define the architecture and guide the way forward.
